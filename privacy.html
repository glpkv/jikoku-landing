Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Short version: Jikoku runs entirely on your Mac and your own iCloud. There are no Jikoku servers, no accounts, and no analytics. We do not collect, transmit, or store your personal content. Period.

1. No data collection

Jikoku has no backend. We do not operate servers that receive your data, we do not require an account or login, and we do not run any tracking or analytics inside the app. Your calendars, reminders, notes, meeting recordings, transcripts, and contacts never leave your device except through Apple’s own iCloud and Exchange sync, which you control.

2. Where your data lives

  • Calendars & reminders are accessed through Apple’s EventKit and synced only via your own iCloud account.
  • Notes are read and written through the Apple Notes app on your Mac; they sync via your iCloud, not through us.
  • Task duration metadata (Jikoku’s addition to reminders) is stored locally on your device.
  • Invite account credentials (e.g. an Exchange or iCloud app password you enter to send invitations) are stored in your Mac’s Keychain and used only to talk directly to those services.

3. On-device AI

Speech recognition, meeting transcription, speaker labelling, note summaries, and task extraction are performed entirely on your Mac using on-device models (WhisperKit and a local large-language model via Apple MLX). Your audio and text are processed locally and are not sent to any cloud service, including Apple Intelligence.

4. System permissions

Jikoku asks for macOS permissions only to provide its features, and only the system, not Jikoku, holds the granted access:

  • Calendar & Reminders — to show and edit your events and tasks.
  • Microphone — for voice dictation and meeting recording.
  • Screen Recording — only if you enable capturing system audio for online calls.
  • Contacts — to suggest guests for invitations and speakers for transcripts. Read locally; never uploaded.
  • Apple Events / Automation (Notes, etc.) — to read and write your notes through the Notes app.

You can review or revoke any of these at any time in System Settings → Privacy & Security.

5. Network use

The only network connections Jikoku makes are directly to the services you configure — Apple iCloud (CalDAV) and your work Microsoft Exchange server — to read calendars and send meeting invitations on your behalf. There is no Jikoku-operated server in between.

6. Children

Jikoku is a general-purpose productivity app and is not directed to children under 13. It collects no data from anyone.

7. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The “last updated” date above reflects the current version. Material changes will be reflected here.
